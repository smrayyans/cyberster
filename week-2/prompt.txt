Week 02 – Task 04
Task 04: Stealth & Firewall Evasion Techniques 
Description: Practice techniques to bypass basic Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) or Firewalls. 
Technical Requirements: 
○ Use Packet Fragmentation (-f) and MTU manipulation to slip past simple packet filters. 
○ Experiment with Decoys (-D) to hide your actual IP address among a crowd of "fake" scanning IPs. 
○ Use Source Port Spoofing (--source-port) to bypass firewalls that only allow traffic from specific ports (like DNS/53). 
Learning Goal: Learn that "Loud" scans get blocked; "Quiet" and "Creative" scans get results.

Technique 1 — Packet Fragmentation (-f)
sudo nmap -sS -f 192.168.0.172
What it does
Splits the scan packets into smaller fragments.
Helps demonstrate how packet inspection systems handle fragmented traffic.
although it will produce same result in metasplotable machine, but its useful in real world

Technique 2 — MTU Manipulation
MTU (Maximum Transmission Unit) controls the size of packets sent over a network.
Changing MTU alters how packets are divided during transmission.
Example Command
sudo nmap -sS --mtu 16 192.168.0.172
{16}
What this means
Forces packets to be very small
Causes the network stack to fragment packets differently

Technique 1 — Decoy Scanning (-D)
Decoy scanning makes it appear as though multiple systems are performing the scan.
Instead of one IP sending packets, Nmap inserts decoy IP addresses along with the real scanner.
